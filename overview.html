What are the job titles for Vision Smith’s ideal jobs? How do each of these rank in terms of demand from employers?

  • - 11. Software Engineer
  • - 16. Senior Software Engineer

The software developer and software engineering titles are listed within the dataset and the job title of Android developer was not. Thus, the title of Android developer could fall under Java developer or software developer.

Having looked at the Burning Glass data, has Vision Smith’s opinion of their ideal job changed? Why or why not?

Jarrod:
My ideal job was to be a senior software engineer at Valve. After looking at the burning glass data, this still has not changed. I would try to build my resume with experience in lower-level software development or software engineering jobs to gain the qualifications required to potentially apply. Where the Burning Glass data does change my opinion, is how I may approach developing my other skill sets, in the event pursuing my ideal job is not feasible. I would try developing a few additional skills that are high in demand, such as SQL, JavaScript, or Java. I could also try expanding my knowledge past the context of integration within games and broaden it to other coding specializations such as a .NET developer. The goal is to maximize my chances of finding a job elsewhere, if pursuing my ideal job at Valve would not work out.

Cameron:
My ideal job was junior software developer; I chose this job to bridge the gap in skills that would be required to further my information technology knowledge and skill sets. My current ideal job has not changed. This job will be a wonderful way to build rapport within the information technology space and will challenge me to continue to improve and grow as a professional in the field.

Derek:
My ideal job was to run my own Mobile applications development business and still nothing has changed from there. I still understand I will need to obtain industry experience before I start my own business and, in that pursuit, I would be looking for a job as at first a junior software developer to gain the skills required and move up the ranks to Senior developer or engineer before moving into my own business.

Kolby:
After looking at the data I don’t see myself changing my opinion on what my ideal job would be. Clearly the biggest problem with being a game developer is the possibility of low pay and having to have an extensive knowledge of multiple fields including 3D modelling, programming, animation, and sound design. The reason being a game developer is intriguing to me is because of the creative freedom and learning opportunities involved in it. Even though the videogame industry doesn’t always pay well I would prefer a career I enjoy over other options with better financial incentives.

Jessica:
Despite the information from the Burning Glass data, I am still happy with my choice to become an Android Developer. For me it's not necessary for the job to be well paying or in high demand, although that is a bonus. My priorities lie with my passion, enjoyment and flexibility of the job itself and I still believe my selected job suits my preferences. If anything, the work I have done to research my ideal job and what the Burning Glass shows me, just solidifies my opinion, and shows me exactly what skills and experience I need to reach my goal.
